STAT 253/317: Syllabus

Cong Ma, University of Chicago, Winter 2026

Schedule

  1. Week 1: Foundations and Basic Examples

    1. Jan 6 (Tue): Lecture 1 — Markov chains; transition probabilities; Chapman–Kolmogorov equations; n-step transitions; simple finite examples

    2. Jan 8 (Thu): Lecture 2 — Communication classes; irreducibility; basic structure of state space; motivating examples (random walk, simple population models)

  1. Week 2: Stationary Distributions and Long-Run Behavior

    1. Jan 13 (Tue): Lecture 3 — Stationary distributions (πP = π); examples; existence and intuition

    2. Jan 15 (Thu): Lecture 4 — Long-run behavior: limiting distributions; ergodic theorem (conceptual); periodicity; when convergence occurs

  1. Week 3: Recurrence/Transience and Reversibility

    1. Jan 20 (Tue): Lecture 5 — Recurrence vs. transience; positive recurrence; expected return times; examples (random walk, absorbing chains)

    2. Jan 22 (Thu): Lecture 6 — Reversible chains; detailed balance; examples and applications (random walk on undirected graphs)

  2. Week 4 Random walk and braching processes

    1. Jan 27 (Tue): Lecture 7 — Generating functions and branching processes

    2. Jan 29 (Thu): Lecture 8 — More on branching processes

  1. Week 5

    1. Feb 3 (Tue): Midterm Exam (covers Lectures 1–8)

    2. Feb 5 (Thu): Lecture 9 — Poisson process; exponential distribution

  1. Week 6

    1. Feb 10 (Tue): Lecture 10 — Thinning, superposition, and related properties

    2. Feb 12 (Thu): Lecture 11 — Continuous-time Markov chains: construction and holding times

  1. Week 7

    1. Feb 17 (Tue): Lecture 12 — Generator matrices; Kolmogorov equations; examples

    2. Feb 19 (Thu): Lecture 13 — Introduction to martingales; conditional expectation; simple examples

  1. Week 8

    1. Feb 24 (Tue): Lecture 14 — Optional stopping (elementary cases): Gambler's ruin

    2. Feb 26 (Thu): Lecture 15 — Brownian motion: definition, basic properties, scaling

  1. Week 9

    1. Mar 3 (Tue): Lecture 16 — Brownian motion as a martingale; reflection principle (intro)

    2. Mar 5 (Thu): Lecture 17 — Stopping times and hitting probabilities for Brownian motion
